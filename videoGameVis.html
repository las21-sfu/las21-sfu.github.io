Click and drag to explore different platforms and sales regions!

Sales Type

Reflection

The visualization reveals distinct regional preferences across gaming platforms. North American sales dominate for most platforms, particularly for Xbox and PlayStation consoles, reflecting strong brand loyalty and market penetration in the region. Japanese sales show pronounced preference for Nintendo platforms and handheld devices, demonstrating the cultural affinity for portable gaming and local brands. European sales patterns closely mirror North American trends but at lower absolute volumes, suggesting similar gaming preferences across Western markets. Other regions contribute modestly across all platforms, indicating developing markets or limited data coverage. The visualization clearly illustrates how platform popularity varies significantly by geography, with Nintendo's stronger performance in Japan versus Microsoft's dominance in Western markets being particularly noteworthy.

Visualization 4: Geographic Appeal and Market Balance of Individual Game Titles in NA, JP, and Global Sales

Click or hover over a dot to highlight it. Move elsewhere to deselect.

NA Sales (Millions)
JP Sales (Millions)

Reflection

I chose a scatterplot because it is the most effective way to simultaneously reveal three variables — NA Sales, JP Sales, and Global Sales — for hundreds of individual game titles at once. The x/y axes directly expose the relationship between two regional markets, making it immediately clear whether a title resonated in one region but not the other (dots hugging the axes) versus titles with balanced cross-regional appeal (dots spread diagonally). Encoding Global Sales as circle size adds a third dimension without cluttering the view, letting the eye naturally group blockbusters from niche titles. I was curious whether high NA performers are simply absent from Japan, or whether there is any overlap at the top — and the scatterplot makes that question answerable at a glance. Each game name is colour-coded so that individual titles remain distinguishable on hover, while the click-to-highlight interaction allows viewers to isolate any single dot and read its exact values through the tooltip without losing the overall distribution for context.
